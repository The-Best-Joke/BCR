WHO WE ARE

The Berkeley College Republicans are a diverse group of independent minds who think critically about issues involving politics, philosophy, and civil service. As the premier political organization at UC Berkeley, we take our role as campus activists and thought leaders seriously.

Though UC Berkeley and its surrounding community have a well-earned reputation of liberalism and progressivism, we feel our presence at this university is critical. Our campus is known as the birthplace of the 1960s Free Speech Movement, and the Berkeley College Republicans embrace and uphold our 1st amendment - we believe that intellectual diversity, academic discouse, and ideological debate are tenets of a college education, and we strive to ensure those components are accessible to students.

We meet in a classroom on campus every Thursday evening during the semester to engage in lively debate (oftentimes over snacks and refreshments), hear from speakers, play politically-themed games, and connect with our likeminded peers. New members are welcome at every meeting and we encourage Republicans, conservatives, Libertarians, moderates, centrists, or anyone questioning their political affiliation to stop by and chat with us; we're always up for healthy discourse!

To many of our club members, BCR is more than a social club or campus organization; it's a community where you can meet lifelong friends.

BCR does it all! We campaign for candidates, hold demonstrations on campus, attend statewide California Republican Party conventions, table each week on Sproul Plaza, host club meetings, participate in campus debates, build an annual 9/11 memorial (on Memorial Glade), work at party fundraisers, throw parties, take trips into SF, and much more!

Through the connections BCR has to offer, many of our members find political internships or paid employment and develop the skills and maturity to carry themselves in high-pressure situations!
